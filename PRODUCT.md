# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Home customers, office workers, and party organizers in Mysuru and Bengaluru who want reliable, authentic Indian home-cooked meals delivered daily. Primary user: a working adult or family who doesn't have time to cook but won't compromise on the quality and taste of home food. They are not ordering an experience — they are solving a daily need. They come back every day.

Secondary users: corporate buyers placing recurring office lunch orders; hosts planning party spreads.

## Product Purpose

Kuteera Kitchen is a daily meal delivery service rooted in home-style South Indian cooking. The landing page's job is to convert a first-time visitor into their first order. Secondary job: communicate that this is a dependable, recurring service (subscriptions), not a one-off food app.

Success: visitor places an order or starts a subscription within the same session.

## Brand Personality

Authentic, Grounded, Caring.

The brand is a trusted home cook, not a startup. Not premium restaurant, not chaotic street food — the quiet confidence of someone who has been cooking the same recipe for twenty years and knows it's good.

Voice: warm but not gushing. Direct but not corporate. Says "home-cooked" and means it.

## Anti-references

- **Swiggy / Zomato**: app-store aesthetics, bright gradient CTAs, discovery-feed energy, gamification. Kuteera Kitchen is not a marketplace.
- **Street food / chaotic market**: overcrowded layouts, poster-style typography, festive-decoration energy. This is calm and domestic, not loud.
- **Fine dining**: white space maximalism, chef-portrait photography, French-restaurant seriousness. Not aspirational luxury — approachable daily staple.
- **Corporate catering sites**: grey-on-white, formal, B2B language, no warmth.

## Design Principles

1. **Home, not hotel** — every decision should feel domestic. If something looks like a restaurant menu app, scale it back.
2. **Restraint as warmth** — avoid maximalist layouts. The calm of a home kitchen, not the noise of a marketplace.
3. **Lead with the meal** — food is the hero. Copy and visuals should make the visitor hungry first, informed second.
4. **Trust through texture** — earthy browns, cream backgrounds, warm photography build more trust than slick gradients.
5. **Reliable, not clever** — this is a daily service. Predictable patterns over surprise interactions. The visitor should never feel lost.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Good practice baseline. No formal WCAG compliance target. Prioritize readable text contrast (aim for 4.5:1 minimum), keyboard navigability on forms, and no critical information conveyed by color alone.
